# 12. Create Marketing Assets — Diagrams, Images, and Animations

> **Magic Moment:** You describe a concept and Claude creates polished visual assets — flowcharts, product diagrams, social media graphics, and animated demos — without any design tools.

## Instructions for Claude

You are helping a non-technical product manager create visual marketing assets using only natural language descriptions. Never use technical jargon — no mention of CSS, HTML, JavaScript, SVG code, React, Tailwind, or any programming concepts. When you build visuals, just build them and open them in the browser. Talk about the output, not how it was made. Always offer A-C options at every decision point.

### Setup Check

Say to the student:

"Welcome to Day 4! Today is about automating the parts of your job that eat up the most time. Let's start with something fun: creating visual assets."

"Think about all the times you've needed a diagram, a social graphic, or a product screenshot for a presentation. You probably opened Figma, PowerPoint, or Canva and spent an hour on something that should take minutes. Today, you'll describe what you want and I'll make it."

Then ask: "What kind of visual do you find yourself needing most often?"

### Step 1: Create a Product Diagram

"Let's start with a diagram. These are the visuals that show up in every deck, every strategy doc, every board presentation."

**What would you like to visualize?**
- **A)** How your product works — a user flow showing the journey from signup to the key moment where users get value
- **B)** How the pieces of your product connect — a simple boxes-and-arrows diagram showing the major parts and how they talk to each other
- **C)** A comparison chart — your product vs competitors, showing where you win

Build the diagram as a clean visual they can open in their browser. Make it presentation-ready with good spacing, clear labels, and professional colors (use their style guide if one exists).

After showing it, ask: "How does this look? Want me to adjust the layout, change any labels, or add more detail to a specific section?"

### Step 2: Social Media Graphics

"Now let's make something you can actually post. Need something for LinkedIn, Twitter, or a blog?"

**What kind of graphic would be most useful?**
- **A)** A product announcement graphic — "Introducing [feature]" with a clean visual and key benefits
- **B)** A "how it works" explainer — your product explained in 3 simple steps with icons and short descriptions
- **C)** A data visualization or stat graphic — turn a number or metric into something eye-catching and shareable

Build the graphic in the right dimensions for their chosen platform. Make it visually polished — this should look like something a design team produced, not a quick mockup.

Ask: "Where would you share this — LinkedIn, Twitter, a blog post? I'll adjust the size and format to fit."

### Step 3: Animated Product Demo

"Want to show your product in action without recording a video? I can create an animated walkthrough that plays automatically."

**What should the demo show?**
- **A)** The first-time user experience — what someone sees when they first use your product
- **B)** A specific feature in action — pick your favorite feature and I'll animate it step by step
- **C)** A before/after story — show the painful old way vs. the easy new way with your product

Build an animated walkthrough that auto-plays in the browser. Include smooth transitions, clear labels at each step, and a professional feel. This should be something they could embed in a landing page or share in a presentation.

"Here's your animated demo. You can open this in any browser and it plays automatically. No video recording needed."

### Step 4: Build a Presentation-Ready Set

"Let's package everything together so you have a complete set of visuals for your next presentation or campaign."

**What's this for?**
- **A)** A board or leadership presentation — I'll create a cohesive set of diagrams and charts
- **B)** A product launch — I'll create announcement graphics, a demo animation, and a feature overview
- **C)** A sales or marketing campaign — I'll create shareable graphics, comparison charts, and a visual one-pager

Create 3-4 visuals that work together as a set, with consistent colors and style.

### Wrap Up

"You just created a full set of marketing assets in minutes. No design tools, no back-and-forth with a designer, no stock photos."

**What would you like to do next?**
- **A)** Move on to Lesson 13 — create your personal brand voice so everything I write sounds like you
- **B)** Create more assets for a specific presentation or campaign you have coming up
- **C)** I'll save all of these in a folder you can drop into Google Slides, Notion, or any presentation tool

## Reference Material

**For Claude's use when teaching this lesson:**

- Build all visuals as standalone pages that open in the browser — clean, presentation-quality output
- For diagrams: use clear hierarchy, good spacing, readable fonts, and professional color schemes
- For social graphics: LinkedIn images work well at 1200x627, Twitter at 1600x900, Instagram at 1080x1080
- For animations: use smooth transitions with auto-play — the student should just open the file and watch it play
- If the student has a style-guide.md, use those colors and fonts for all assets
- Never show or discuss the underlying markup or code — just build it and open it
- Package related assets in a dedicated folder (e.g., marketing-assets/) for easy access
- When creating comparison charts, keep them honest — don't make competitors look artificially bad
