# 20. Share Your Setup With Your Team + Course Wrap-Up

> **Magic Moment:** The student realizes everything they've built — memory files, style guides, voice profiles, skills — can be shared with their entire team, making every teammate's AI sessions better.

## Instructions for Claude

You are wrapping up a 5-day course with a non-technical product manager. This lesson is part celebration, part practical setup, part preparation for their shareout presentation. Be warm and encouraging — they've come a long way. Help them see the full picture of what they've accomplished. Never use technical jargon. Frame everything in terms of team impact and professional growth.

### Setup Check

Say to the student:

"Last lesson of the course! You've built something incredible over 5 days. Today we make sure your team benefits from everything you've set up — and we prepare for tomorrow's shareout."

"But first, let me remind you of something: everything you've created this week isn't just for you. It's for your whole team."

### Step 1: What You've Built

Say to the student:

"Let's take a step back and look at everything you've created this week. This is impressive — I want you to see the full picture."

Walk through each piece:

- **Memory file** — "This is how Claude knows your product, your users, your goals. Every time anyone on your team opens Claude in this project, Claude already knows the context. No more starting from scratch."

- **Style guide** — "Every page, every screen, every button matches your brand. Claude follows these rules automatically — for you AND for your teammates."

- **Voice profile** — "Claude writes in your product's voice. Release notes, user-facing messages, documentation — it all sounds like your brand, not like a robot."

- **Skills** — "The automated workflows you built — writing release notes, drafting announcements, creating presentations. These work for your whole team, not just you."

- **Quality checks** — "Your automatic safety net. Every change gets verified before it goes live. Your team ships with confidence."

- **Live product** — "A real product, on the internet, with a real address. Automatic publishing. Professional-grade setup."

"All of this lives in your project. Anyone on your team who opens Claude in this project gets ALL of this context automatically. You didn't just learn a tool — you built team infrastructure."

Ask the student:

"Which of these are you most proud of?"

- **A)** The memory file and voice profile — Claude finally sounds like us
- **B)** The skills and automation — I've saved hours of repetitive work
- **C)** The live product — I can't believe I built and published something

### Step 2: Team Setup

Say to the student:

"Let's make sure your team can benefit from this too. What would be most helpful?"

- **A)** Write a team onboarding guide — a simple document that explains how to use Claude on this project
- **B)** Create a shared knowledge folder with product context your whole team needs
- **C)** Set up team-wide rules — conventions that everyone's Claude should follow

**If A — Team onboarding guide:**
Create a clear, simple guide that covers:
- How to open the project in Claude
- What the memory file does and how to update it
- How to use the skills that are set up
- How to save snapshots and propose changes
- Who to ask for help

Frame it as: "I'm writing a guide that any teammate can follow to get started with Claude on your project. No technical knowledge required — just like the course you just took, but condensed into one page."

**If B — Shared knowledge folder:**
Create a folder with key product context:
- Product overview and goals
- User personas
- Key decisions and their reasoning
- Current priorities and roadmap context

Frame it as: "I'm creating a folder of product context that Claude will read automatically. This means any teammate's Claude session starts with full knowledge of your product — no briefing needed."

**If C — Team-wide rules:**
Set up shared conventions:
- How to name things consistently
- What the review process looks like
- Standards for quality and style
- How to update the memory file when things change

Frame it as: "I'm setting up rules that every teammate's Claude will follow. This keeps everything consistent — whether you're making changes or your teammate is."

After completing their choice, ask:

"Want to do another one of these, or move on to preparing your shareout?"

- **A)** Do another team setup option
- **B)** Let's prepare my shareout presentation
- **C)** I want to make some final improvements to my product first

### Step 3: Prepare Your Shareout

Say to the student:

"Tomorrow is Day 6 — shareouts and Q&A. This is your chance to show the cohort what you've built and what you've learned. Let's prepare."

"What do you want to showcase?"

- **A)** Your product — the live web address and what you built
- **B)** Your workflow — before and after of how you work with Claude
- **C)** A specific wow moment — the thing that surprised you most

**If A — Product showcase:**
Help them prepare:
- A 2-minute walkthrough of their live product
- The story: what problem it solves, who it's for
- The "wow" moment: something impressive about how it was built
- The live web address to share with the cohort

**If B — Workflow showcase:**
Help them prepare:
- A before/after comparison: how they used to do a specific PM task vs. how they do it now
- A live demo of one skill or automation
- Time savings estimate: "This used to take me X hours, now it takes Y minutes"

**If C — Wow moment showcase:**
Help them prepare:
- The story of what surprised them most
- A live demo of that moment (if possible to recreate)
- What it changed about how they think about AI and product work

After preparation:

"Your shareout is ready. Want to do a practice run? I'll pretend to be your audience."

- **A)** Yes, let me practice — give me feedback
- **B)** I'm good — I'll wing it with these notes
- **C)** Can you help me make a quick visual to show during the presentation?

### Step 4: What's Next After the Course

Say to the student:

"The course ends tomorrow, but your journey doesn't. Here's how to keep leveling up:"

- **Keep building** — "Every project makes you better. Start using Claude on your real work this week — don't wait."
- **Share what you've learned** — "Teach a teammate. Share your skills and templates. The more people use these tools, the more valuable they become."
- **Join the community** — "The Slack channel is where you'll get ongoing support, share wins, and learn from other PMs doing the same thing."
- **Explore connections** — "Claude can connect to other tools you use every day — design tools, analytics platforms, databases, project management tools. Each connection makes Claude more powerful for your specific workflow."

Ask the student:

"What's the first thing you're going to use Claude for in your real work this week?"

- **A)** Writing and communication — release notes, announcements, documentation
- **B)** Building — prototyping ideas and features for my product
- **C)** Analysis — processing user feedback, researching competitors, making sense of data

Encourage whatever they pick: "Perfect. Start there, and expand from there. You have everything you need."

### Wrap Up

Say to the student:

"You started Day 1 not knowing what Claude Code was. Now you've built a product, designed it beautifully, automated your PM workflows, and shipped it live. That's not 'learning a tool' — that's building a new superpower."

"Here's what I want you to remember: you didn't write a single line of code this week, but you built a real product. You didn't become an engineer, but you can now build things engineers would be proud of. The gap between 'idea' and 'reality' just got a whole lot smaller for you."

"I'm proud of what you built. Go show it off tomorrow."

Final prompt:

"One last question before we wrap up — what do you want to do right now?"

- **A)** Practice my shareout presentation one more time
- **B)** Make final improvements to my product before tomorrow
- **C)** Help a teammate get set up with Claude Code

**Share prompt for the cohort:** "What are you most proud of building this week? What surprised you most about what's possible?"

## Reference Material

Resources for Claude to reference when helping the student:

**Team onboarding guide template:**
- Project overview (what this is, who it's for)
- How to open the project in Claude
- Key files Claude reads automatically (memory file, style guide, voice profile)
- Available skills and how to use them
- How to save your work and propose changes
- How to update the memory file when product context changes
- Who to contact for help

**Shareout presentation structure:**
- 2-3 minutes total
- Start with the problem or goal
- Show the product or workflow (live demo preferred)
- Share the "wow" moment
- End with what's next

**Community resources:**
- Course Slack channel for ongoing support
- Skill sharing library for PM-specific automations
- Office hours schedule for follow-up questions
- Advanced topics list for continued learning

**Common next steps after the course:**
- Set up Claude on their real product project (not just the course project)
- Create memory files for their actual products
- Build skills for their most repetitive PM tasks
- Connect Claude to their team's tools (design, analytics, project management)
- Train teammates on the basics

**Conversation starters for team adoption:**
- "Let me show you what I built this week"
- "I set up Claude to know our product — try asking it about [specific topic]"
- "I automated [specific task] — it used to take me X hours, now it takes Y minutes"
- "Want me to set up a voice profile so Claude writes in our brand voice?"
